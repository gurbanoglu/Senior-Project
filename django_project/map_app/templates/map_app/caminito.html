Camanito

Caminito is a colourful street museum in Buenos Aires. Despite
having the name "caminito" which can mean "little walkway" in
Spanish, this location was originally a stream. Eventually the
water stream dried up, and the path became a rail line. In 1954,
the rail line was removed and the path turned into a landfill, but
by 1959 an artist by the name of Benito Quinquela Martin began
rebuilding the walkway with the intention of honouring the place
where residents in Buenos Aires had used as a port for immigration.

Benito Quinquela Martin had created the street name "Caminito"
with the goal of representing the old immigrant barrio called.
La Boca which is where the street Caminito is located within.
Despite being a tourist area, La Boca remains a working-class
neighborhood exhibiting a strong Italian flavour as many of its
first residents had migrated from the northern Italian city of
Genoa. Street performers, dancers, and musicians, who express
the dance of Tango are a regular occurence. The name "Caminito"
has roots to a Tango in 1926 practiced by La Boca musician Juan
de Dios Filiberto. Surrounding the street museum are restaurants
that serve Italian pizza, and shops that sell handmade crochet
scarves and shawls.
