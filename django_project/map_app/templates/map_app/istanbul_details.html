İstanbul


Customs
  • Entry requires that you register a visa which comes with a one-time fifty-dollar fee.
       Visa approvals normally occur in three business days or less.

  • Mosques are not open to the entire public during prayer sessions. Only worshippers
       can attend during this time.

  • Women who plan to go inside a mosque must cover their heads, and cannot wear
       short-length or loose-fitting clothing.

  • Both men and women are supposed to have their legs covered when inside a mosque.

  • The mosques in İstanbul provide head coverings for women, leg coverings for all, and
       an area for storing your shoes before going inside.

  • If you are young and all other seats on public transportation are filled up, vacate your
       seat for pregnant women, the elderly, and disabled.

  • Turkish men will sometimes greet eachother by bumping their foreheads on the right
       side and then the left.

  • Men only shake hands, hug, or kiss unrelated women if the women have initiated
       these kinds of salutations first.

    • Faux Pas
  • It is forbidden to enter a mosque with shoes, or any other kind of footwear on.

  • If you are in a mosque, do not walk too close to someone who is praying. Remember
       to use your inside voice.

  • Remove your shoes upon setting foot in someone's home. Some hosts may offer you
       indoor slippers.

  • Do not clap or cheer during a whirling dervish ceremony because it is not a performance.

  • Do not refer to Turkish people as "Arabs" because they are of a different ethnicity,
       and speak a different language than them.

    • Safety
  • Drivers do not always obey the rules of the road, so be observant when crossing streets.

  • Do not go to a bar, club, or restaurant with strangers. Some locals pretend to befriend
       tourists only for the purpose of forcing them to pay for an inflated price.

  • In the case of a shoeshiners throwing their brush onto the street, never pick it up even
       if they ask you to. This is a dishonest trick that shoeshiners attempt on tourists.

  • Putting it in perspective, İstanbul has a lower level of crime than New York City, and
       ranks slightly better on the safety index.
