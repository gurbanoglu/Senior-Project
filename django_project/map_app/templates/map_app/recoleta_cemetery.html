Recoleta Cemetery

"It is cheaper to live a whole life of luxury than to be buried at
the Recoleta." This saying is rooted in the fact that the elite of
Buenos Aires have honoured the deceased with lavish sculptures inside
the "Cementerio de la Recoleta". Many of Argentina's famous polticians,
celebrities, and nobel prize winners. Arguably the most prominent figure
buried here is former first lady Eva Perón who passed away in 1952, but
wasn't moved to the Recoleta Cemetery until 1976 due to political turmoil.
A two hour tour is available for ten US dollars per person, but does not
include the entrance ticket which is an additional fee. The cemetery has
been regarded as one of the world's best cemeteries by BBC because of its
marble mausoleums, city-like walkways, and neo-gothic architectural style.

Fourteen acres occupied by over 6,400 coffins, statues,
sarcophagi, and crypts acknowledge the men and women who
contributed to Argentine society. French civil engineer
Próspero Catelin designed the cemetery which then became
his final resting place. In 1822, the cemetery opened and
was established as the first public cemetery in Buenos Aires.
The land which the cemetery has been built upon had belonged
to Franciscan Recollect monks which explains how the cemetery
was given its name. The most popular sites at the cemetery
are the resting places of Eva Perón and former Argentine
presidents Domingo Faustino Sarmiento and Raúl Alfonsín.
