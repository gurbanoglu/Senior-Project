  • Fortunately for Western visitors, Turks use a latin-based alphabet which they
       switched to 1928. A prime reason for the switch is due to the fact that the latin-based
       script is better suited for the Turkish language because it permits the usage of the
       eight long and short Turkish vowels. In contrast, the Arabic alphabet only consists of
        three long and short vowels, making it a poor choice.

       English speakers will have the capability of making out some Turkish street names,
       but can cause confusion when attempting to enunciate words containing the following
       as these letters do not exist in English: “ç,” “ı,” “ğ,” “ş,” “ö,” and “ü.”.

    • Transportation
  • The most suitable method of getting around İstanbul, is by purchasing a rechargeable
       "Anonymous Istanbulkart" which costs about 72 US cents. This card will cover the all
       of the public transportation options in İstanbul except for shared taxis (Dolmuş). It is
       suggested that each passenger loads about $1.50 on their card since each trip usually
       costs 25 US cents on average.

  • The trams on the European side run from 6 a.m. to 11 p.m. while trams on the Asian
       side operate from 7 a.m. to 9 p.m.

  • If you wish to cross the Bosporus Strait by boat, a public ferry (vapur) is available
       every twenty minutes until 11 p.m. This is not a recommended way to sightsee the
       Bosporus because of the large crowds of locals. A gratifying way to intake the
       immensity of this city that sits at the crossroads of East and West is by using the
        Bosporus Cruise tour costing only 70 US cents per adult.

  • A shared taxi or minibus where each passenger pays only a portion of the normal fee
       is called a "Dolmuş", but it only departs when all or nearly all the seats are occupied.

       Payments are made in cash to the driver. These minibuses can be sought at their
       departure points for a certain route. You are supposed to pay the same amount as
       what other passengers are paying (typically $1-$1.7).

  • All official taxis in İstanbul will have digital meters. Avoid commuting with taxis that
       do not turn on their meter.
