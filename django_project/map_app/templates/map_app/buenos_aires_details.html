Buenos Aires


Customs
  • Have enough cash on hand as many businesses do not accept cards for payment.

  • When using public transportation, if you are sitting down and all other seats are
       occupied, you are expected to vacate your seat for pregnant women, children, and
       the elderly.

  • It is not uncommon for Argentines to kiss strangers when they have just met. If you
       are kissed, you are expected to return one, otherwise it is seen as impolite.

  • Like many South Americans, Argentines drink a caffeine-rich infused drink known
       as "maté". Argentines will consume their national beverage, maté, by sharing it in a
       single cup.

    • Faux Pas
  • Avoid talking about the Falkland islands or as Argentines refer to the them: "Islas Malvinas".
       Argentina has a recurring sovereignty dispute with the United Kingdom regarding these
       islands which resulted in a two-month war between the two countries in 1982.

  • Asking questions is fine, but do not speak negatively of the former president
       Juan Perón as he is adored throughout Argentina.

  • It is better not to bring up the topic of Nazis fleeing to Argentina after WW2. Or conspiracy
       theories regarding this.

  • Fernet is a highly popular liquor drink in Argentina, so even if you don't like it, be sure not
       to excessively complain about it.

    • Safety
  • Buenos Aires is among the safer cities in South America.

  • The largest concern is pickpocketing and bag-snatching which is why caution and alertness
       is advised when in crowded public transportation and ferias (street markets).

  • It is not recommended to use your phone while you are outside in Buenos Aires, but to
       instead access it while you are inside a cafe, shop, or restaurant.

  • It is advised not to expose yourself by wearing expensive jewelry and if not needed, leave
       your passport and high amounts of cash in your hotel room.

  • Firmly refuse help from random individuals if they approach you, point out a stain of any
       type on your clothing, and then offer to clean it off.

  • Do not allow a random person or driver on the street to assist you with checking or
       exchanging the cash you have on hand.
