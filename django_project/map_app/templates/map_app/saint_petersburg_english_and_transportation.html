English Proficiency & Transportation


English Proficiency
  • Although some basic English proficiency can be found in Saint
       Petersburg, keep in mind that only 13% of Russians have
       some knowledge in English.

  • It is recommended to learn some essential words before arriving:
       Basic Russian Vocabulary for Travelers

  • Russians use the Cyrillic alphabet which was given to their ancestors
       in the ninth century by a Greek monk named Cyril. Those who can
       only read a latin alphabet will not be able to read street signs in Saint
       Petersburg unless they are shown in latin.

    • Transportation
  • Whilst in Saint Petersburg, it is a unique experience for tourists to ride
       the deepest underground metro in the world. Not simply for its effective
       transportation, but primarily because of its palace-like appearance.

  • The metro runs seven days a week from 5:40 a.m. to 12:30 a.m.

  • Fares for a single journey cost about $1, but if you will be in Saint
       Petersburg for over a week, you are better off purchasing a travel card
       where you decide how much credit you want to put on it.

  • Privately owned mini-buses called "Marshutkas" where each roams the
       city following a fixed route is a secondary option costing a little over $1
       for a single ride per person.

  • Do not go with a random person who appears to be a taxi driver as they may
       overcharge you. Instead request a taxi by accessing the mobile apps of Yandex
       Gett, or Taxovichkoff where the payment process is similar to Uber.
