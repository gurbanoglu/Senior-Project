English Proficiency
  • Basic English proficiency can be found at hotels and is more
       common among young people in Buenos Aires.

  • It is less common for those who work in public transportation
       to speak English.

  • It is worth noting that only 15% of Argentines can conduct a
       basic conversation in English. Although Argentina has its own
       Spanish dialect, the following are some useful Spanish phrases

  • In spite of the survival of several indigenous languages, Spanish
       is the predominant language in Argentina.

    • Transportation
  • Using the underground metro or the "subte" as the locals refer
       to it, is the most convenient way of exploring the city.

  • The subte operates on all weekdays, but stops running at 11 p.m.

  • Subte ticket cost: $0.25

  • To avoid getting scammed, only buy metro tickets at the subte
       stations.

  • Buses run twenty-four-seven throughout Buenos Aires.

  • Bus fare cost: $0.25 (Exact change must be brought.)
